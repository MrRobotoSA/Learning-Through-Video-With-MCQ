Summary

1. Traditional societies: were how humans lived in the distant past.
People provided for there own needs.
People used nature to gather resources.
They survived in the wild and where self-sufficient

2. Bartering: means to exchange something that is your posession for something else
As people moved around the world they traded with different groups of people

3. Promissionary notes, coins and paper money
3.1) Promissionary notes are documents that have been
signed by all parties involved to confirm an agreement.
3.2) South Africa's first paper money came into circulation
in the 1780s with the introduction of the Rixdollar.
3.3) Metals have long been used as metal substance in the
form of pieces or bars. Every time when trade took place,
they had to be weighed and tested. it was stil very inconvenient,
and then people came up with the plan to make metal pieces of a
set weight with some or other design engraved upon it. This was
the beginning of coin money.

4. Electronic banking
4.1) When you use a debit card, smartcard or do online shopping,
we refer to it as e-commerce
4.2) SEP (secure electronic payment) the facility for safe electronic payment
used to pay for services offered and goods delivered to the customer.

5. Needs and wants

A need:
can be defined as something without which we cannot go.
We need it to survive
Limited: means there is only a certain number of resources, goods, etc.
Unlimited: means there is no end to the resource, goods needs, etc.
Limited resources and unlimited needs lead to scarcity.
Scarcity is a big problem in SA because not all needs can be met.

A want: can be defined as something that we realy desire,
but do not necessarily need to survive

6. Goods and services

6.1) Producers refer to people (Workers) or businesses that produce goods in order to supply these goods to businesses like supermarkets. who then sell the goods to consumers to satisty their needs.

6.2) Goods refer to things that we can see and feel

6.3) A service is something that is provided by a person, e.g. a hairdresser. Your hair is Cut for you, thus a service is offered.

6.4) Consumers refer to the people who buy and use the produced goods. We can all be seen as consunmers Because we daily Use goods and services to satisfy our needs.
